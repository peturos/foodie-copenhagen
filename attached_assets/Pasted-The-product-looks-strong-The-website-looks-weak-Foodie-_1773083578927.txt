The product looks strong. The website looks weak.
Foodie already has real business substance to work with: a physical location on Amagerbrogade, a phone number, online ordering, a broad menu, vegetarian/vegan/gluten-free options, and a clear quality angle around fresh sourdough bread. The site itself shows all of that, but it presents it in a dated, low-trust, low-conversion way.

The biggest issues are not subtle. Multiple pages still use the generic title “Your Webpage Title,” the homepage pushes users into a long wall-of-text menu, the “View Full Menu” link goes to a 404, and the opening hours on the main site do not match the ordering flow. The main site says Monday–Friday 11:00 am–07:00 pm and Saturday 12:00 am–04:00 pm, while the ordering system shows Monday–Friday 11:00–18:00 and Saturday 11:30–15:00. That kind of inconsistency kills trust fast.

Also, the current structure is upside down. Foodie is selling sandwiches and quick takeaway, but the site gives prominent space to pages like Apply for Job, a weak About Us page, a basic Gallery, and a contact form that says “Order Your Food Here” instead of clearly pushing people to the ordering platform. The brand feels more like an old template than a popular neighborhood food business.

What Foodie should become

Foodie’s website should do 4 things:

Make the food look irresistible

Make ordering effortless

Make the brand feel local, popular, and trustworthy

Help new customers decide in under 10 seconds

That is the whole game.

Positioning for the redesign

Foodie should be positioned as:

“A local Amager favorite for premium sandwiches, fresh bread, and fast takeaway.”

Not “#1 take away restaurant.”
That claim is generic and weak unless they can prove it. Better to sell what is actually believable:

freshly baked sourdough

standout sandwiches

local pickup/delivery

vegetarian/vegan/gluten-free options

trusted neighborhood spot

That is already supported by what they currently say about the bread and menu.

What is wrong with the current site
1. It does not sell visually

From your screenshots, the hero image is dark, unclear, and storefront-led instead of food-led. For a sandwich business, that is the wrong first impression. The food should be the hero, not the facade.

2. It makes the menu feel harder than it should

The homepage drops users into a very long text menu with mixed Danish/English naming and minimal structure. That might contain information, but it does not create appetite.

3. It creates trust issues

The broken menu link, generic metadata, awkward hour formatting, and conflicting opening hours all signal neglect. People may still order, but new customers will hesitate.

4. It wastes navigation space

Top-nav items should be built around customer intent:
Menu, Order, About, Find Us, Catering
Not Apply for Job as a primary destination. That belongs in the footer.

5. It undersells the differentiators

The best copy currently on the site is the bread section. That should be a selling point on the homepage, not buried between template-style sections.

Menu page strategy

This page matters a lot.

Right now the ordering platform is doing the real commerce work, while the website is acting like a messy brochure. The new website should become a clean decision-making layer before the user clicks into checkout.

The menu page should have:

category tabs at the top

strong item photography

short, standardized descriptions

dietary tags: V / VG / GF option

prices

“Order now” deep links

Do not make users read a giant block of items like the current homepage does.

Copy direction

Foodie needs simpler, tighter, more confident language.

The current copy is mixed-language and sometimes awkward, for example the About page text and phrases like “Order Your Food Here” on the contact form.

The tone should be:

warm

local

appetizing

direct

premium but unpretentious

Example homepage copy

Fresh sandwiches. Proper bread. Big flavor.
At Foodie on Amagerbrogade, we bake our sourdough fresh and fill it with bold ingredients, from pesto chicken and Egyptian kebab to falafel, avocado, and baba ganoush. Order online for pickup or delivery.

That is much stronger than the current copy style.
